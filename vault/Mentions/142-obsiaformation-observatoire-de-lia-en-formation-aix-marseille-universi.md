## Summary

An analysis piece on ObsiaFormation, the official AI-in-education observatory of Aix-Marseille Université (AMU), published in its "veille" (monitoring/watch) section under the ANALYSES category. The article frames the May 2026 resolution of an Erdős conjecture by an autonomous AI as a paradigm shift, then situates it within the ethical framework of the 2026 Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics (Déclaration de Leyde). It explains the declaration's concerns about AI-generated proofs (integrity, author attribution, research-agenda capture by tech companies, peer-review standards, access inequality), notes it recommends principles for individual mathematicians plus guidance for organizations and policymakers with emphasis on open-source infrastructure, and states the declaration is supported by the International Mathematical Union.
