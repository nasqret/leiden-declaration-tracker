# Mathematician Bryna Kra Among Authors of International Declaration on AI and Mathematics

> **Outlet:** [[Weinberg College News, Northwestern University]] · **Type:** 🏛️ Institutions & societies · **Date:** 2026-06-02 · **Language:** English · **Country:** [[United States (country)\|United States]]

**Link:** https://news.weinberg.northwestern.edu/2026/06/02/mathematician-bryna-kra-among-authors-of-international-declaration-on-ai-and-mathematics/

## Summary

Northwestern's Weinberg College news announces the release of the 2026 Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics, highlighting that Northwestern mathematician Bryna Kra is one of 16 authors. It outlines five core threats AI poses to mathematics (flawed proofs burdening peer review, erosion of attribution, inequity in research access, overhyping of results, and loss of disciplinary autonomy to commercial interests) and notes the declaration grew from a September 2025 Lorentz Center workshop, with a working group spending roughly six months drafting it.

## Key quote

> Mathematicians must be part of shaping these changes rather than merely reacting to them. This declaration is a call for such engagement. (Bryna Kra)

## Significance

Institutional outlet (Northwestern University / Weinberg College news), so reach is primarily within the university and academic community rather than mass-market. However, it is notable because the subject, Bryna Kra (Sarah Rebecca Roland Professor of Mathematics, former president of the American Mathematical Society, member of the U.S. National Academy of Sciences), is a prominent mathematician and one of the declaration's 16 authors who served on the drafting working group. This corroborates the declaration's authorship and institutional uptake.
