# 给AI立规矩，AI刚破80年数学难题，数学家们慌了

> **Outlet:** [[36氪 (36Kr)]] · **Type:** 📰 Newspapers & wire services · **Author:** ASI启示录 · **Date:** 2026-06-05 · **Language:** Chinese · **Country:** [[China (country)\|China]]

**Link:** https://www.36kr.com/p/3839947197958400

## Summary

Chinese tech/business news article on the 36Kr platform reporting on OpenAI's AI cracking the 80-year-old Erdős unit-distance problem and the mathematical community's resulting alarm. It explicitly describes the 2026 Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics (《莱顿人工智能与数学宣言》): about 60 leading mathematicians and computer scientists signing at Leiden University, worried that mathematics is shifting from "public knowledge" to a "commercial black box." It lays out the declaration's three principles — disclosure rights (transparent disclosure of AI use in papers), verification rights (commercial AI must not monopolize the interpretation of truth), and autonomy (math should be driven by curiosity, not by compute profitability). Notes 650+ signatories as of June 3, and references Fields medalist Timothy Gowers's reaction.
