# 150+ Mathematicians Warn Governments Not to Believe AI Hype

> **Outlet:** [[ICO Optics]] · **Type:** ✍️ Blogs · **Author:** ICO Optics (no individual byline) · **Date:** 2026-06-07 · **Language:** English · **Country:** [[United States (country)\|United States]]

**Link:** https://www.ico-optics.org/150-mathematicians-warn-governments-not-to-believe-ai-hype/

## Summary

The article reports on the 2026 Leiden Declaration on AI and Mathematics, describing how 150+ mathematicians warn governments that excitement about AI's ability to solve hard mathematical problems exceeds what has been scientifically proven. It cites IMU vice president Ulrike Tillmann and Leiden University's Rodrigo Ochigame, and summarizes the declaration's concerns about AI generating persuasive-but-incorrect proofs, flawed proofs slipping into peer review, commercial hype, and broader ethical/environmental risks.

## Key quote

> AI can help with computation and pattern-spotting... But jumping from helping to proving on its own? That's a massive leap.
