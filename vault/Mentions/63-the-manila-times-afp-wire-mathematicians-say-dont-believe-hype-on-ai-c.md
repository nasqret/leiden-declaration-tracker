# Mathematicians say 'don't believe hype' on AI capabilities

> **Outlet:** [[The Manila Times (AFP wire)]] · **Type:** 📰 Newspapers & wire services · **Author:** Agence France-Presse (AFP) · **Date:** 2026-06-04 · **Language:** English · **Country:** [[Philippines (country)\|Philippines]]

**Link:** https://www.manilatimes.net/2026/06/04/news/world/mathematicians-say-dont-believe-hype-on-ai-capabilities/2358208

## Summary

AFP wire story, republished by The Manila Times, reporting on the 2026 Leiden Declaration in which mathematicians warn governments and the public not to "believe the hype" about AI's mathematical abilities. It explains the declaration's concerns that AI could produce plausible-but-incorrect proofs hard for humans to verify, short-circuit peer review, and undermine attribution/credit, and that the future of mathematical research must remain guided by human judgment.

## Key quote

> The declaration notes "there is currently a strong commercial incentive on the part of the technology industry to overstate the capabilities of their products."
