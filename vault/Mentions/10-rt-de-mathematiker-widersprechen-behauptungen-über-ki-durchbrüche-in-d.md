# Mathematiker widersprechen Behauptungen über KI-Durchbrüche in der Forschung

## Summary

A German-language news article from RT DE (the German-language service of RT/Russia Today, a Russian state-controlled international broadcaster) reporting on the Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics. The piece frames the declaration as an appeal ("Aufruf an Regierungen" - call to governments) by 150+ leading mathematicians from many countries, urging the scientific community and governments NOT to treat the grandiose claims of AI developers as genuine mathematical breakthroughs. It explicitly attributes the declaration to collaboration with the International Mathematical Union and the University of Leiden, matching the 2026 declaration (not the 2015 Leiden Manifesto for research metrics). The article emphasizes the declaration's core warnings: AI systems can produce plausible-looking arguments that turn out to be erroneous, false proofs are hard to distinguish from correct ones before expert review, and technology companies have a financial interest in overstating their products' capabilities. It also relays the declaration's demands around attribution, transparency/open science, peer review of AI-assisted papers, and researchers retaining responsibility for correctness. The reported trigger is a wave of reports claiming large language models solved problems mathematicians had worked on for decades.

## Key quote

> Mathematiker aus aller Welt rufen die Regierungen dazu auf, im Umgang mit dem Hype um künstliche Intelligenz vorsichtig zu sein.
