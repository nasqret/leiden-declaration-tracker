# The world's mathematicians just issued a formal declaration telling AI companies to stop using their work without permission

> **Outlet:** [[The Next Web (TNW)]] · **Type:** ✍️ Blogs · **Author:** Alina Maria Stan · **Date:** 2026-06-02 · **Language:** English

**Link:** https://thenextweb.com/news/leiden-declaration-ai-mathematics-proof-attribution

## Summary

TNW reports on the IMU-endorsed Leiden Declaration on AI and Mathematics, framing it as mathematicians formally telling AI companies to stop using their published work without permission. It explains the declaration was developed over eight months by a 17-member working group and outlines five threats AI poses to mathematics: unreliable AI-generated proofs, improper attribution of human work in training data, distorted research priorities, results bypassing peer review via press releases, and threats to mathematical autonomy; it also lists recommendations for mathematicians, organizations, and policymakers, and notes signatories including Peter Scholze.

## Key quote

> Mathematics is, and should always remain, a profoundly human endeavour. (Ulrike Tillmann, IMU vice president, as quoted in the article)
