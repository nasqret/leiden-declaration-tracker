## Summary

A Zhihu column post reproducing a Chinese-language full text / translation of the 2026 Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics. It frames the declaration as a historic act of collective self-rescue by the mathematics community, noting it was officially released by the International Mathematical Union (IMU) on 2 June 2026, grew out of the September 2025 Lorentz Center workshop ("Mechanization and Mathematical Research") at Leiden University with ~60 researchers from 10 countries, and was signed by hundreds of mathematicians including Fields Medal winners. It summarizes the declaration's warning that uncontrolled AI automation threatens core mathematical values: the certainty of proof, author responsibility, third-party verifiability, community evaluation standards, and mathematicians' autonomy to shape research questions.

## Key quote

> 数学研究的核心价值在于证明带来的高度确定性、作者的责任、第三方的可验证性、社区的评价标准，以及自主塑造研究问题的能力。 (The core values of mathematical research lie in the high certainty brought by proof, the responsibility of authors, third-party verifiability, the community's evaluation standards, and the ability to autonomously shape research questions.)
