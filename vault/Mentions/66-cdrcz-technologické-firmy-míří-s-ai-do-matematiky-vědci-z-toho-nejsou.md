# Technologické firmy míří s AI do matematiky. Vědci z toho nejsou dvakrát nadšení

> **Outlet:** [[cdr.cz]] · **Type:** 🧪 Science & tech magazines · **Author:** Lukáš Čihák (Francesco) · **Date:** 2026-06-05 · **Language:** Czech · **Country:** [[Czechia (country)\|Czechia]]

**Link:** https://cdr.cz/clanek/technologicke-firmy-miri-s-ai-do-matematiky-vedci-z-toho-nejsou-dvakrat-nadseni

## Summary

Czech tech outlet article on the tension between tech companies deploying AI in mathematics and the research community's concerns. It centers on the Leiden Declaration on AI and Mathematics, noting it was published 2 June 2026, developed over eight months by 16 researchers following a September 2025 Leiden University conference, and endorsed by the International Mathematical Union. Concerns covered include AI-generated false proofs, unauthorized use of published work in training data, weak attribution, and premature announcements bypassing peer review (citing OpenAI's geometry proof claims).

## Key quote

> AI modely dokážou vytvářet texty, které vypadají jako přesvědčivé matematické důkazy, ale mohou být chybné. (AI models can generate texts that look like convincing mathematical proofs, but may be erroneous.)

cdr.cz is an established Czech technology news magazine, providing Czech-language coverage of the declaration. Mid-tier regional/national tech outlet reach; extends the declaration's visibility into the Czech-language audience.
