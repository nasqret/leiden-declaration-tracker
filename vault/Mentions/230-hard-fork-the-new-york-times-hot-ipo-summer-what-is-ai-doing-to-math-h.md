# Hot I.P.O Summer + What Is A.I. Doing to Math? + HatGPT

> **Outlet:** [[Hard Fork (The New York Times)]] · **Type:** 🎙️ Podcasts · **Author:** Kevin Roose and Casey Newton · **Date:** 2026-06-05 · **Language:** English · **Country:** [[United States (country)\|United States]]

**Link:** https://www.nytimes.com/2026/06/05/podcasts/hardfork-ai-math-ipo.html

## Summary

Episode of the New York Times technology podcast Hard Fork, hosted by Kevin Roose and Casey Newton. One of the three segments covers the declaration signed by more than 1,000 (per the declaration itself, 2,500+) mathematicians raising concerns about the use of AI in their field. Guest Kevin Hartnett, author of "The Proof In the Code: How a Truth Machine is Transforming Math and AI," joins to explain the controversy. A host characterizes the declaration as "a very worried document," and Hartnett describes mathematicians as "deeply worried" about a "massive exogenous force" disrupting a discipline that has self-regulated for centuries. The episode explicitly discusses the Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics (confirmed via Michael Harris's Silicon Reckoner Substack analysis of the episode). Other segments cover the SpaceX/Anthropic/OpenAI IPO wave and a HatGPT news round including a new executive order on AI.

## Key quote

> A host called it "a very worried document," and Hartnett described mathematicians as "deeply worried" by "this like massive exogenous force" disrupting a community that "largely was able to run itself and self-regulate for centuries."
