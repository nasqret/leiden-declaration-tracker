# Why hundreds of mathematicians have backed a declaration against unchecked AI use

> **Outlet:** [[The Indian Express]] · **Type:** 📰 Newspapers & wire services · **Date:** 2026-06 · **Language:** English · **Country:** [[India (country)\|India]]

**Link:** https://indianexpress.com/article/technology/artificial-intelligence/why-mathematicians-signed-leiden-declaration-ai-10721732/

## Summary

Explainer article in The Indian Express (technology/AI section) on the Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics, the June 2026 statement led by Jim Portegies and endorsed by the International Mathematical Union. The piece explains why the international group of mathematicians backed the declaration, covering concerns about unchecked automation threatening the practice and values of mathematics: unreliable/hard-to-verify AI-generated proofs, lack of transparency and delayed peer review by tech companies (e.g. DeepMind's AlphaProof taking over a year to publish methods), unauthorized use of mathematicians' work to train AI without consent, skewed research priorities favoring showcase results, and ethical concerns about military/surveillance applications. It notes the declaration's recommendations (disclose AI use, ensure peer review, level the playing field between academia and for-profit companies via legal resources and public funding), its origin at the September 2025 Lorentz Center workshop in Leiden, and that it is open for signatures and to be discussed at the ICM in Philadelphia.

## Key quote

> The Leiden Declaration cautions that unchecked automation threatens not only how math is practiced, but what the discipline stands for.
