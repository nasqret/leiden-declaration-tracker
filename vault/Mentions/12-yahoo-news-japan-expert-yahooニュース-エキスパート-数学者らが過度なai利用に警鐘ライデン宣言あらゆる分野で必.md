## Summary

A Yahoo News Japan "Expert" opinion column by tech/AI commentator Sato Jin reporting on the 2026 Leiden Declaration on AI and Mathematics. It explains that the declaration, signed by 150+ mathematicians and backed by the IMU, does not seek to exclude AI from mathematics but to establish clear rules protecting the reliability and autonomy of the field, demanding transparency and accountability for AI-assisted results (researchers taking responsibility and disclosing AI use). It extends the warning beyond mathematics, arguing that in law, medicine, education, policy and corporate analysis, AI output used without human verification can be treated as if it were expert judgment.

## Key quote

> ライデン宣言の意義は、AIを研究から排除することではなく、AIを使った成果に透明性と責任を求めた点にある (The significance of the Leiden Declaration lies not in excluding AI from research, but in demanding transparency and responsibility for AI-assisted results.)
