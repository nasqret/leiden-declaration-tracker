# Mathematicians call for AI regulation, warn of research integrity crisis

> **Outlet:** [[Rappler]] · **Type:** 📰 Newspapers & wire services · **Date:** 2026-06 · **Language:** English · **Country:** [[Philippines (country)\|Philippines]]

**Link:** https://www.rappler.com/technology/mathematicians-leiden-declaration-ai-regulation-research-integrity/

## Summary

Rappler reports on the 2026 Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics, describing how nearly 2,000 mathematicians (from institutions including Harvard, Columbia, Cambridge and Oxford) signed it to call for oversight and ethical use of AI in math research. It explains the declaration's ethical framework asserting the primacy of human autonomy, its warning that AI produces "plausible but unreliable (or even incorrect)" arguments, and concerns over attribution and copyrighted work, alongside recommendations that individual mathematicians disclose AI use and remain accountable, and that policymakers protect authors' rights, regulate the AI industry, and invest in public computational infrastructure.

## Key quote

> The declaration warns that AI produces "plausible but unreliable (or even incorrect)" arguments that are difficult to distinguish from correct proofs.
