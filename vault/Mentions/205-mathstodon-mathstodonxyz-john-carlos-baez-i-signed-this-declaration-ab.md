# John Carlos Baez: "I signed this declaration about AI in mathematics, and you might also want to"

> **Outlet:** [[Mathstodon (mathstodon.xyz)]] · **Type:** 💬 Social media · **Author:** John Carlos Baez · **Date:** 2026-06-02 · **Language:** English · **Country:** [[United Kingdom (country)\|United Kingdom]]

## Summary

A Mastodon post by mathematical physicist John Carlos Baez on Mathstodon (a Mastodon instance for mathematicians). Baez announces that he signed the Leiden Declaration on AI in mathematics and links directly to leidendeclaration.ai, encouraging others to sign too. He then offers a critique: he finds the declaration's passage on AI disturbing the "current system of incentives" a bit complacent, arguing the existing incentive system is already seriously flawed (citing university administrators, journal oligopolies, and the military) and should be improved rather than merely defended against AI disruption. This is an original first-party post by a signatory, explicitly referencing this 2026 declaration (not the 2015 Leiden Manifesto).

## Key quote

> I signed this declaration about AI in mathematics, and you might also want to: https://leidendeclaration.ai/ ... Our current system of incentives is seriously flawed, so we should be working to improve it, not merely defending the status quo.
