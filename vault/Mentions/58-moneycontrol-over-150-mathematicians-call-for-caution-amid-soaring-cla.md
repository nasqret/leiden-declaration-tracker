# Over 150 mathematicians call for caution amid soaring claims about AI breakthroughs

> **Outlet:** [[Moneycontrol]] · **Type:** 📰 Newspapers & wire services · **Author:** Moneycontrol News · **Date:** 2026-06-09 · **Language:** English · **Country:** [[India (country)\|India]]

**Link:** https://www.moneycontrol.com/world/over-150-mathematicians-call-for-caution-amid-soaring-claims-about-ai-breakthroughs-article-13944202.html

## Summary

Moneycontrol (India's leading business/financial news portal) reports that more than 150 mathematics experts worldwide endorsed the 11-page "Leiden Declaration on AI and Mathematics," urging policymakers not to overestimate AI's ability to solve difficult mathematical problems. The piece explicitly cites the declaration by name, notes IMU vice president Ulrike Tillmann's accompanying statement, and situates it against recent AI hype: a 23-year-old's ChatGPT "Erdős problem" claim and OpenAI's claim to have disproved the 80-year-old "unit distance" conjecture. It quotes the declaration on commercial incentives to overstate AI capabilities, advises consulting mathematicians over press releases, and references concerns about training on scholarly work without consent and broader harms (military/surveillance use, misinformation, environmental costs). It names Leiden University AI anthropologist Rodrigo Ochigame among those involved. This is an original Moneycontrol News write-up (crediting Futurism as a source for framing), not a verbatim republication.
