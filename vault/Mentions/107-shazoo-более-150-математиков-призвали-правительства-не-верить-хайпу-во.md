# Более 150 математиков призвали правительства не верить хайпу вокруг возможностей ИИ

> **Outlet:** [[Shazoo]] · **Type:** ✍️ Blogs · **Author:** Коэн (Cohen) · **Date:** 2026-06-12 · **Language:** Russian

**Link:** https://shazoo.ru/2026/06/12/185471/bolee-150-matematikov-prizvali-pravitelstva-ne-verit-shumikhe-vokrug-vozmozhnostei-ii

## Summary

Russian-language news/entertainment site Shazoo reports on the Leiden Declaration on AI and Mathematics, stating that over 150 mathematicians worldwide signed it to urge governments not to believe exaggerated claims (hype) about AI's ability to solve complex mathematical problems. It conveys the declaration's core message that the future of mathematical research must be guided by human judgment and transparent practices, warns that the tech industry has commercial incentives to overstate product capabilities, and notes that current automated methods can produce plausible but unreliable reasoning.

## Key quote

> Будущее математических исследований должно определяться человеческим суждением, честными и прозрачными практиками. (The future of mathematical research must be guided by human judgment and honest, transparent practices.)

## Significance

Shazoo is a popular Russian gaming/tech/entertainment news blog with a sizable general-interest audience, but it is not a specialist science or mathematics outlet and not academically authoritative. The byline "Коэн (Cohen)" is a Shazoo staff handle, not a notable mathematician or AI researcher. Value is as evidence of broad Russian-language / general-public reach of the declaration. The article correctly cites confirmed signatories Ulrike Tillmann (IMU Vice President) and Leslie Ann Goldberg (Oxford).

## Notes

Confirmed as referencing THIS 2026 Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics (not the 2015 Leiden Manifesto for research metrics). Headline and framing match wider June 2026 coverage (e.g., Yahoo/The Next Web "Over 150 mathematicians warn governments not to believe the hype about AI"). Named figures Tillmann and Goldberg are verified declaration supporters. Note minor framing imprecision: article cites "150+" signatories whereas the declaration ultimately had 2,500+; this likely reflects an early signatory count at time of writing (12 June 2026).
