# AI bedreigt wiskunde, waarschuwen onderzoekers

## Summary

Dutch online business/media/marketing news outlet Emerce reports that an international group of mathematicians has issued the Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics, warning that AI threatens the core values of their discipline. The initiative began at a Leiden University workshop; a group of sixteen researchers led by Jim Portegies (mathematician at TU Eindhoven) prepared the final text. The article lists five key risks the declaration identifies: unreliable outputs, missing source attribution, dependence on closed commercial systems, inflated/hyped claims, and loss of scientific independence. Named signatories include former Education Minister Robbert Dijkgraaf and Fields medalist Terence Tao. The declaration does not call for an AI ban but urges transparency about AI use, careful attribution, human accountability, and stricter government regulation of the AI sector; it is endorsed by the International Mathematical Union (IMU). It notes Portegies will present the declaration at the ICM in Philadelphia on 26 July 2026.
