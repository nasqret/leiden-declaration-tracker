# OpenAI resolveu um enigma matemático com 80 anos, mas cientistas criticam processo

> **Outlet:** [[Público (Portugal)]] · **Type:** 📰 Newspapers & wire services · **Author:** Sérgio Magno · **Date:** 2026-06-03 · **Language:** Portuguese · **Country:** [[Portugal (country)\|Portugal]]

**Link:** https://www.publico.pt/2026/06/03/enter/noticia/openai-resolve-enigma-80-anos-matematicos-criticam-processo-2176965

## Summary

The article reports OpenAI's claim that an internal AI model solved an ~80-year-old Erdős combinatorial-geometry problem (the unit distance problem), then frames scientists' criticism of the opaque, corporate process. It cites the 2026 "Declaração de Leiden sobre Inteligência Artificial e Matemática" (Leiden Declaration on AI and Mathematics) as the community's response, noting it grew from a Leiden University workshop and warns against proprietary AI systems exploiting freely published mathematics without transparency, and calls for transparency, regulation, and not treating AI as a coauthor.

## Key quote

> "A empresa não divulgou nada sobre os métodos, os comandos escritos por humanos, os dados de treino ou os recursos computacionais consumidos." (The company disclosed nothing about methods, human-written prompts, training data, or computational resources consumed.) — attributed to declaration co-author Rodrigo Ochigame.

## Significance

Público is one of Portugal's two leading national daily newspapers, giving the declaration significant mainstream Portuguese-language reach. The article ties the declaration to a topical OpenAI news hook and quotes declaration co-author Rodrigo Ochigame, plus references mathematician Melanie Matchett Wood (Harvard). Author Sérgio Magno is a tech journalist, not a notable mathematician/AI figure.
