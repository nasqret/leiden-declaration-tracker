# Leiden Declaration: AI is challenging the core values of mathematics

> **Outlet:** [[Leiden University]] · **Type:** 🏛️ Institutions & societies · **Author:** Leiden University (no individual byline) · **Date:** 2026-06-02 · **Language:** English

**Link:** https://www.universiteitleiden.nl/en/news/2026/06/leiden-declaration-warns-ai-is-challenging-the-core-values-of-mathematics

## Summary

Leiden University's official news announcement of the Leiden Declaration on AI and mathematics, an international statement by a working group of 16 researchers warning that AI threatens core mathematical values such as proof verification, attribution and research integrity. It notes the declaration emerged from the September 2025 NIAS-Lorentz workshop, was convened by Jim Portegies (TU/e), and is officially supported by the International Mathematical Union, urging clear community norms rather than an AI ban.

## Key quote

> The goal of mathematical research is human understanding of mathematics, so mathematics can only thrive in a community of human researchers. — Peter Scholze

## Significance

High institutional significance: this is the host university's own announcement of the declaration, making it a primary/authoritative source. Leiden University is a major research institution, and the piece quotes notable mathematician Peter Scholze and cites IMU Vice President Ulrike Tillmann's endorsement, lending substantial credibility and reach.

## Notes

Confirmed as referencing THIS 2026 Leiden Declaration on AI and mathematics (not the 2015 Leiden Manifesto for research metrics): page explicitly names the September 2025 NIAS-Lorentz workshop, Jim Portegies of Eindhoven University of Technology, the working group of 16 authors, and IMU support. Article does not mention the 2,500+ signatory count or the Zenodo DOI, and does not list Terence Tao among signatories, but core subject identity is unambiguous.
