## Summary

Columbia University's news outlet covers the 2026 Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics, featuring Columbia math professor Michael Harris, who co-authored it. It describes the declaration as proposing guidelines for appropriate use of AI while reaffirming mathematics' core values, with recommendations addressed to individual mathematicians, mathematical societies, policymakers, and industry; it notes the declaration was signed by 2,100+ mathematicians, endorsed by the International Mathematical Union, and backed by figures such as Peter Scholze and Terence Tao.

## Key quote

> Mathematics is more than a machine for producing correct answers—it's a deeply human endeavor built on creativity, understanding, collaboration, and the pursuit of knowledge for its own sake. These values often clash with the incentives driving AI development.
