# AI threatens math, researchers warn

> **Outlet:** [[Eindhoven University of Technology (TU-e)]] · **Type:** 🏛️ Institutions & societies · **Date:** 2026-06-03 · **Language:** English

**Link:** https://www.tue.nl/en/news-and-events/news-overview/03-06-2026-ai-threatens-math-researchers-warn

## Summary

TU/e's official news page reports on the 2026 Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics, drawn up by a working group of sixteen international researchers convened by TU/e mathematician Jim Portegies, growing out of a 2025 Lorentz Center workshop at Leiden University. It outlines five main risks AI poses to mathematics (unreliable results, missing citations, dependence on closed commercial systems, exaggerated claims, and loss of scientific independence), notes IMU endorsement and signatories such as Terence Tao, and frames the declaration as a call for responsible use rather than a ban.

## Key quote

> Who is responsible for any errors, and who gets the credit if the argument is correct?
