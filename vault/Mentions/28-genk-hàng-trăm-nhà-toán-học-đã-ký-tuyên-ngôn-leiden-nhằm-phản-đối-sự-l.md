## Summary

Vietnamese tech-news article reporting that hundreds of mathematicians (650+ cited) have signed the 2026 Leiden Declaration to oppose the misuse of AI in mathematical research. It names signatories such as Fields Medalist Peter Scholze and co-author Rodrigo Ochigame (Leiden University), and outlines the declaration's concerns about transparency, authorial responsibility, intellectual property (models trained on mathematicians' work), and AI-generated errors spreading through peer review like "a virus."

## Key quote

> Rodrigo Ochigame, an anthropologist and historian of computing and AI at Leiden University, a co-author of the declaration, states that in announcements like those from OpenAI, "essential basic information needed to assess the scientific significance of results is kept secret."
