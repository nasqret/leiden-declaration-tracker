# Leiden Declaration warns AI threatens mathematics' core values

> **Outlet:** [[MSN (en-xl regional-international edition, syndicated)]] · **Type:** 🔗 Other · **Date:** 2026-06 · **Language:** English · **Country:** [[United States (country)\|United States]]

## Summary

Syndicated MSN news article reporting on the 2026 Leiden Declaration on AI and Mathematics, issued by scholars from 15 universities across five countries (US, UK, Netherlands, Poland, Switzerland) and endorsed by the International Mathematical Union. It explains that the declaration warns AI could disrupt the core values of mathematics (proof, verification, research autonomy), overload peer review with AI-generated papers, and raises ethical concerns about commercialized AI models. It notes the declaration grew out of the September 2025 "Mechanization and Mathematical Research" workshop in Leiden and is expected to be discussed at the next International Congress of Mathematicians.

## Key quote

> Some AI models are being commercialized for uses that raise serious ethical concerns, including warfare, oppression, mass surveillance, and the erosion of democracy. Many mathematicians did not anticipate their research being used for such purposes.
