# Leiden Declaration: Why Mathematicians Want Consent for AI Training

> **Outlet:** [[Pebblous (blog)]] · **Type:** ✍️ Blogs · **Author:** Pebblous editorial · **Date:** 2026-06-02 · **Language:** English · **Country:** [[South Korea (country)\|South Korea]]

**Link:** https://blog.pebblous.ai/blog/leiden-declaration-ai-mathematics/en/

## Summary

A corporate blog post by Pebblous, a South Korean AI-data-quality deeptech startup, analyzing the Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics. It correctly describes the declaration as emerging from discussions among 60+ mathematicians at Leiden University's Lorentz Center in September 2025, formalized (published June 2, 2026) and led by Jim Portegies with 16 mathematicians from 15 universities. It frames the declaration as a response to OpenAI's press-release announcement of an AI disproving an 80-year-old Erdős conjecture without peer review, and lists its three core objections: using papers as training data without author consent, bypassing peer review via press releases, and failing to attribute prior work. It notes 1,000+ signatures within 24 hours and endorsement by the International Mathematical Union and Fields Medalist Peter Scholze. The post connects the "consent for AI training" theme to Pebblous's own data-quality/AI-ready-data business perspective.

## Key quote

> The output of mathematicians who never imagined their work would be used to develop AI is being used for exactly that purpose, without consent.
