# Ponad 150 ekspertów od matematyki ostrzega, że rządy mogą przeceniać realne możliwości AI

> **Outlet:** [[GRYOnline.pl]] · **Type:** 📰 Newspapers & wire services · **Author:** Zbigniew Woźnicki · **Date:** 2026-06-08 · **Language:** Polish · **Country:** [[Poland (country)\|Poland]]

**Link:** https://www.gry-online.pl/newsroom/ponad-150-ekspertow-od-matematyki-ostrzega-ze-rzady-moga-przeceni/z33109f

## Summary

A Polish-language tech/gaming news article reporting that over 150 mathematicians worldwide published and signed the "Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics" to warn governments against overestimating AI's real capabilities and against drawing their AI knowledge from corporate promises and press releases rather than consulting specialists. It notes the AI industry's PR use of a single success (an AI solving an Erdős problem) despite AI's tendency to produce undetectable hallucinations, and raises concerns about models being trained on mathematicians' work without consent and underfunding of mathematics education.

## Key quote

> Zdaniem naukowców władze państw powinny unikać czerpania wiedzy w temacie AI z obietnic korporacji.

## Notes

Confirmed via direct WebFetch of the page and corroborating search snippet; both explicitly name the "Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics" (2026), so the false-positive guard (2015 Leiden Manifesto) does not apply. The headline says "over 150" experts; the actual signatory count is far higher (2,500+), but the article clearly concerns this declaration.
