# Over 150 Mathematicians Warn Governments Not to "Believe the Hype" About AI

> **Outlet:** [[Yahoo News (Science) — syndicated from Futurism]] · **Type:** 📰 Newspapers & wire services · **Author:** Victor Tangermann · **Date:** 2026-06-06 · **Language:** English · **Country:** [[United States (country)\|United States]]

**Link:** https://www.yahoo.com/news/science/articles/over-150-mathematicians-warn-governments-100000243.html

## Summary

News article reporting on the 2026 Leiden Declaration on AI and Mathematics, in which mathematicians warn governments and the public not to overstate AI's capabilities in mathematics. It explains that AI can produce plausible but unreliable or incorrect proofs, that there is a commercial incentive to overstate product capabilities, and that human judgment and the shared values of the mathematical community must guide the field; it urges governments not to let AI breakthrough claims drive research funding or policy.

## Key quote

> "Current automated techniques can produce plausible but unreliable (or even incorrect) arguments which are difficult to distinguish from correct mathematical proofs."

## Significance

Yahoo News is a major high-traffic aggregator, and this piece is syndicated from Futurism, a widely-read science/tech outlet, giving it broad mainstream reach. Author Victor Tangermann is a staff journalist at Futurism (not a mathematician or AI researcher). The article cites International Mathematical Union vice president Ulrike Tillmann and declaration co-drafter Rodrigo Ochigame (Leiden University), lending it institutional weight.

## Notes

Confirmed as the 2026 Leiden Declaration on AI and Mathematics (not the 2015 Leiden Manifesto for research metrics): the article quotes the declaration's actual text, references the IMU, and discusses AI-generated proofs, peer review integrity, and unauthorized use of mathematicians' work for AI training. Original source is Futurism (https://futurism.com/artificial-intelligence/mathematicians-warn-governments-hype-ai); Yahoo is a republication. The article references "over 150" prominent signatories rather than the 2,500+ total; it does not name Terence Tao. Date 2026-06-06 is the Yahoo timestamp; the declaration itself was published 2026-06-02.
