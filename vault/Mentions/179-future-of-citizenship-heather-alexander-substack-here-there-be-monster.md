# Here There Be Monsters

> **Outlet:** [[Future of Citizenship (Heather Alexander, Substack)]] · **Type:** 📨 Newsletters · **Author:** Heather Alexander · **Date:** 2026-06-14 · **Language:** English · **Country:** [[Canada (country)\|Canada]]

**Link:** https://futureofcitizenship.substack.com/p/here-there-be-monsters

## Summary

A Substack essay on the "Future of Citizenship" newsletter by Heather Alexander, a Montreal-based researcher (CRE Montreal Centre for Research in Ethics; co-director of the Laboratory for the Future of Citizenship). The piece uses mathematics as a community-based cultural practice (Erdos number, collaboration) as a lens to examine how AI is encroaching on the math world, prompted by AI's recent solution of a long-standing Erdos problem. It explicitly names and characterizes the Leiden Declaration on AI and mathematics, summarizing its concerns: LLM unreliability, shallow results, loss of trust, tech-industry intrusion, the death of peer review, and the AI personhood problem. This is the 2026 declaration, not the 2015 Leiden Manifesto on metrics.

## Key quote

> The Leiden Declaration is focused on the concerns of the math world today: LLM unreliability, shallow results, a loss of trust, tech industry intrusion
