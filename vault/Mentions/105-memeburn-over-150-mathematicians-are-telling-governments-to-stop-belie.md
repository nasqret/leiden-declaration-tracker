## Summary

The article reports on the 2026 Leiden Declaration, in which 150+ mathematicians urge governments to stop trusting AI math-breakthrough claims when shaping research funding and policy. It lays out the declaration's core warnings: AI can produce convincing 'proofs' with nearly invisible errors, fails to properly attribute or cite human work, and cannot replace human mathematical judgment; it frames recent hype (e.g., OpenAI's May 2026 claim of disproving an 80-year Erdos conjecture) as exactly the pattern the declaration warns about.
