# Sixteen mathematicians have published the 'Leiden Declaration,' warning of potential threats to mathematics, including the accuracy and reliability of AI.

> **Outlet:** [[GIGAZINE]] · **Type:** ✍️ Blogs · **Author:** GIGAZINE (no individual byline) · **Date:** 2026-06-03 · **Language:** English · **Country:** [[Japan (country)\|Japan]]

**Link:** https://gigazine.net/gsc_news/en/20260603-leiden-declaration-on-ai-and-math/

## Summary

English-edition GIGAZINE article reporting that sixteen mathematicians published the Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics, warning that AI poses five distinct threats to mathematical research: plausible-but-false proofs, attribution/copyright issues, technological inequality, overhyped press coverage of AI results, and loss of research autonomy. It notes the declaration advocates tool-disclosure transparency, human responsibility for correctness, formal peer review, and institutional AI-use policies.

## Key quote

> mathematics is not merely an accumulation of results, but a human endeavor that fosters understanding, clarity, and judgment

## Significance

GIGAZINE is a high-traffic Japanese technology/news site (this is its English edition), giving the declaration reach into Japanese and international tech audiences. The article correctly attributes the project to Jim Portegies (Eindhoven University of Technology), cites the IMU endorsement and IMU Vice President Ulrike Tillmann, and traces the origin to the September 2025 Lorentz Center workshop at Leiden University.
