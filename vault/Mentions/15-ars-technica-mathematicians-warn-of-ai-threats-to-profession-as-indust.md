# Mathematicians warn of AI threats to profession as industry encroaches

> **Outlet:** [[Ars Technica]] · **Type:** 📰 Newspapers & wire services · **Date:** 2026-06-02 · **Language:** English · **Country:** [[United States (country)\|United States]]

**Link:** https://arstechnica.com/tech-policy/2026/06/mathematicians-warn-of-ai-threats-to-profession-as-industry-encroaches/

## Summary

Ars Technica reports on the Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics, the IMU-endorsed statement (with 1,800+ signatories at time of writing) warning that recent AI developments threaten core values of mathematical research such as reliable proof, attribution, and peer review. It highlights concerns about AI producing plausible-but-incorrect proofs, growing tech-industry influence over the discipline's autonomy, and exploitation of published papers as training data.

## Key quote

> AI models can "produce plausible but unreliable (or even incorrect) arguments which are difficult to distinguish from correct mathematical proofs." The declaration tells policymakers to "don't believe the hype," warning that tech companies have "a strong commercial incentive... to overstate the capabilities of their products."
