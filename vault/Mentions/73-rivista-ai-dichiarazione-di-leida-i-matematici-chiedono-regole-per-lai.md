## Summary

Italian-language coverage by the AI-news magazine Rivista AI of the 2026 Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics. The article reports that the declaration, published by 16 researchers from 15 European and American universities and supported by the International Mathematical Union, grew out of a September Lorentz Center workshop at Leiden University (~60 researchers, 10 countries), and outlines five threats AI poses to mathematics (unreliable/erroneous reasoning, attribution and copyright violations, dependence on costly AI tech, overstated AI-capability communication, and loss of research autonomy to corporate interests), calling for transparent disclosure of AI use, sustained human responsibility for correctness, and government policy frameworks.
