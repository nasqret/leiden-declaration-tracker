# Leiden Declaration on AI and Mathematics

> **Outlet:** [[LinkedIn — Norbert Gehrke]] · **Type:** 💬 Social media · **Author:** Norbert Gehrke · **Date:** 2026-06-09 · **Language:** English

## Summary

A LinkedIn post by fintech commentator Norbert Gehrke describing the Leiden Declaration, a statement by researchers on AI's impact on mathematics that emerged from the September 2025 Leiden University workshop (60 scholars, 10 countries) and was published in June 2026. The post summarizes the declaration's five major risks (unreliable results entering publications, attribution/copyright failures, dependence on proprietary tools and expensive compute, overhyping of AI results, and loss of mathematics' autonomy over its research agenda) and notes its call for norms on transparency, peer review, human responsibility, and industry relationships, as well as its endorsement by the International Mathematical Union.

## Key quote

> The declaration calls for the establishment of norms regarding transparency, peer review, human responsibility, and relationships with industry to utilize AI while upholding core mathematical values.
