# Matemáticos testam IAs em Harvard e modelos são aprovados em 7 dos 10 problemas

> **Outlet:** [[Olhar Digital]] · **Type:** 📰 Newspapers & wire services · **Author:** Layse Ventura · **Date:** 2026-06-14 · **Language:** Portuguese · **Country:** [[Brazil (country)\|Brazil]]

**Link:** https://olhardigital.com.br/2026/06/14/inteligencia-artificial/matematicos-testam-ias-em-harvard-e-modelos-sao-aprovados-em-7-dos-10-problemas

## Summary

The article reports on the "First Proof" initiative in which 30 mathematicians at Harvard tested four AI systems against ten unsolved problems, with the models (mainly OpenAI's GPT-5.5 Pro and Google's Gemini 3.1 Pro Preview) solving seven of ten. It notes that in parallel mathematicians released the "Declaração de Leiden," an international declaration with 2,300+ signatories setting ethical guidelines for AI use in mathematics, recognizing AI's potential while flagging risks that models fail to credit the ideas they use and that companies promote successes without transparency about failures.

## Key quote

> A declaração reconhece o potencial da tecnologia, mas aponta riscos: os modelos não creditam as ideias que utilizam, e as empresas promovem sucessos sem transparência sobre os casos de falha.
