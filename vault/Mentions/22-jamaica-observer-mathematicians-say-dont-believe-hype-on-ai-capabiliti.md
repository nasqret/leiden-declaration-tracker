# Mathematicians say 'don't believe hype' on AI capabilities

> **Outlet:** [[Jamaica Observer]] · **Type:** 📰 Newspapers & wire services · **Author:** Agence France-Presse (AFP) · **Date:** 2026-06-02 · **Language:** English · **Country:** [[Jamaica (country)\|Jamaica]]

**Link:** https://www.jamaicaobserver.com/2026/06/02/mathematicians-say-dont-believe-hype-ai-capabilities/

## Summary

An AFP wire report on the 2026 Leiden Declaration, describing how mathematicians signed a declaration warning governments and the public not to "believe the hype" about AI's mathematical abilities, citing commercial incentives that drive AI labs to exaggerate reasoning capabilities. It raises the declaration's concerns about unverifiable AI-generated proofs, undermined attribution, peer-review timelines being replaced by corporate publicity, and trend-chasing research.

## Key quote

> There is a competition to the death on the part of the main labs... they are using mathematics... to attract investment so that each of them will be left standing. — Michael Harris, Columbia University, declaration co-author

## Notes

Confirmed reference to THIS 2026 Leiden Declaration on AI and mathematics (Leiden University / Lorentz Center origin, IMU-endorsed, signatories including Terence Tao). Not the 2015 Leiden Manifesto for research metrics. This is a syndicated AFP article; the page reports 150+ signatories (the AFP framing) rather than the later 2,500+ total. Verified by direct WebFetch of the URL plus corroborating search snippets identifying AFP as the source.
