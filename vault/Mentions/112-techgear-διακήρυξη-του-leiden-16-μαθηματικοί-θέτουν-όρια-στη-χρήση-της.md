## Summary

Greek-language tech news article reporting on the Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics, drafted by 16 mathematicians to set ethical and scientific standards for AI use in mathematical research. It covers the declaration's recommendations on accountability for algorithmic errors, attribution/IP, transparency in peer review and independent verification of proofs, and notes the backing of the International Mathematical Union, framing the goal as protecting science and human oversight rather than banning AI.

## Key quote

> Allowing algorithms to produce theorems no one can verify transforms mathematics from exact science into an act of faith.

## Notes

WebFetch of the live URL succeeded and confirmed the article references THIS 2026 declaration (IMU endorsement, verification/peer review/attribution themes). Not the 2015 Leiden Manifesto. Article cites 23 recommendations across three axes and over 130 signatories at time of writing.
