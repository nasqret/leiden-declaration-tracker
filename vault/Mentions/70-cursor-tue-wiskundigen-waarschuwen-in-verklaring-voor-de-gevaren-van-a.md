# Wiskundigen waarschuwen in verklaring voor de gevaren van AI

> **Outlet:** [[Cursor (TU-e)]] · **Type:** 🏛️ Institutions & societies · **Author:** Lydia van Aert · **Date:** 2026-06-08 · **Language:** Dutch

**Link:** https://www.cursor.tue.nl/nieuws/2026/juni/week-2/wiskundigen-waarschuwen-in-verklaring-voor-de-gevaren-van-ai

## Summary

Cursor, the independent news outlet of Eindhoven University of Technology (TU/e), reports on the Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics, highlighting that TU/e assistant professor Jim Portegies guided the writing process. The article lays out the declaration's five shared concerns about AI threatening mathematics (unreliable results, poor citation practices, lack of methodological transparency, dependence on commercial AI systems, and growing inequality/reduced researcher autonomy), traces its origins to a Cambridge conference conversation and the September workshop in Leiden, and notes IMU endorsement plus signatories such as Terence Tao and Robbert Dijkgraaf.

## Key quote

> De snelle opmars van AI bedreigt de wiskunde. (The rapid advance of AI threatens mathematics.)

## Significance

Cursor is the official/independent journalistic outlet of TU/e, the home institution of working-group lead Jim Portegies, giving this an authoritative insider angle on the declaration's authorship. As a university news source it is a niche/institutional outlet rather than national mainstream press, but it carries weight within the academic community. The author, Lydia van Aert, is a Cursor staff journalist, not a mathematician or AI figure.

## Notes

False-positive guard passed: the article clearly references the 2026 Leiden Declaration on AI and Mathematics (Jim Portegies/TU/e, September Leiden/Lorentz workshop, IMU endorsement, Tao and Dijkgraaf signatories, ICM Philadelphia presentation), not the 2015 Leiden Manifesto for research metrics. Confirmed via direct WebFetch of the page and corroborating WebSearch results. Minor discrepancy: this article cites 1,500+ signatories whereas the task brief states 2,500+, likely reflecting growth over time since publication (8 June 2026); the TU/e English release is dated 3 June 2026.
