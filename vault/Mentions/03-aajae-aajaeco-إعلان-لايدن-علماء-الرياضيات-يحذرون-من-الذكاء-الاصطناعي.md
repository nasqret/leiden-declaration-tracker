## Summary

Arabic-language article reporting on the 2026 Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics, describing how a group of mathematicians from leading universities issued the declaration to frame the debate over AI's impact on mathematics. It warns that AI can produce arguments that look reasonable but are unreliable or incorrect and hard to distinguish from genuine proofs, and cautions against corporate hype, threats to mathematics' autonomy, and unequal collaboration between researchers and tech companies; it situates this against AI breakthroughs such as OpenAI's late-May 2026 refutation of an 80-year-old Erdős conjecture.

> حجج تبدو معقولة لكنها غير موثوقة (أو حتى غير صحيحة) ويصعب تمييزها عن البراهين الرياضية الصحيحة

## Significance

aajae.co is a minor Arabic news aggregator; the piece is a republication of an article originally carried by Al-Araby Al-Jadeed (The New Arab, alaraby.co.uk), a significant pan-Arab outlet.
