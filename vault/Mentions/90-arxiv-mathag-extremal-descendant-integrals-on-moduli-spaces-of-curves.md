# Extremal descendant integrals on moduli spaces of curves: An inequality discovered and proved in collaboration with AI

> **Outlet:** [[arXiv (math.AG)]] · **Type:** 📄 Preprints · **Author:** Johannes Schmitt · **Date:** 2025-12-16 · **Language:** English · **Country:** [[United States (country)\|United States]]

**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.14575

## Summary

A research-level algebraic geometry preprint by Johannes Schmitt determining extremal values of pure psi-class (descendant) intersection numbers on moduli spaces of stable curves, where the central inequality was discovered and proved in collaboration with AI models (GPT-5, Gemini 3 Pro, Claude Opus 4.5, Claude Code, GPT-5.2), with portions formalized in Lean. The acknowledgments section explicitly references the 2026 Leiden Declaration: the author thanks the members of the writing group of the "Leiden declaration on the use of AI in mathematical research," naming Jarod Alper, Michael Harris, David Holmes, Ursula Martin and Jim Portegies (the declaration's lead) for conversations about AI use in mathematics and feedback on drafts. This is an explicit, in-the-wild scholarly reference to the Leiden Declaration, not the 2015 Leiden Manifesto on metrics. Note: the Leiden mention appears in the paper's acknowledgments (visible via the arXiv HTML version), not in the abstract; submitted 16 Dec 2025 with comments "14 pages; comments are very welcome!".

## Key quote
