# Leiden Declaration on AI and Mathematics

> **Outlet:** [[FDLIST (Bielefeld mathematics forum, Discourse)]] · **Type:** 🗣️ Forums & discussion · **Author:** fdlist.team (on behalf of Karthik Ganapathy, SEW Postdoctoral Fellow, UC San Diego) · **Date:** 2026-06-05 · **Language:** English · **Country:** [[Germany (country)\|Germany]]

**Link:** https://fdlist.math.uni-bielefeld.de/t/leiden-declaration-on-ai-and-mathematics/373

## Summary

FDLIST is the Discourse-based discussion forum hosted by the mathematics department at Bielefeld University (a long-running community board for the mathematics research community, originally an offshoot of the Functional Differential List / math mailing-list culture). In a thread posted on 5 June 2026 in the General Discussion category, the forum (fdlist.team, relaying a message from postdoctoral fellow Karthik Ganapathy of UC San Diego) brings the Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics to the attention of the mathematics community. The post explicitly describes the declaration as a document drafted over roughly eight months by mathematicians, computer scientists, and historians that "sets out shared concern about the use of AI in mathematical research." It notes that the declaration is endorsed by the International Mathematical Union and had gathered around 1,500 signatures at the time of posting, and it encourages colleagues to read the text and consider signing. The framing is participatory advocacy rather than reporting: the forum is mobilizing fellow mathematicians to engage with and endorse the declaration. This is unambiguously the 2026 declaration (AI in mathematical research, IMU endorsement) and not the 2015 Leiden Manifesto for research metrics.

## Key quote

> a group of mathematicians, computer scientists, and historians drafted the Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics, which sets out shared concern about the use of AI in mathematical research.
