## Summary

Arabic-language popular-science article ("AI Stirs Controversy in the World of Mathematics: Ally or Adversary?") that explicitly discusses the "Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics" (إعلان لايدن حول الذكاء الاصطناعي والرياضيات). Opening with OpenAI's reported solution to the unit-distance problem, it frames AI as a double-edged tool in mathematics, then summarizes the Declaration's core warnings: AI can produce plausible-looking but unreliable or wrong proofs that are hard to distinguish from correct ones; tech companies' goals (showcasing model power) diverge from scientists' goals; and AI systems systematically exploit mathematicians' work without rights, raising copyright concerns. It notes the Declaration does not reject AI but calls for transparent, responsible use, with humans retaining ultimate scientific responsibility. Published approximately early June 2026.
