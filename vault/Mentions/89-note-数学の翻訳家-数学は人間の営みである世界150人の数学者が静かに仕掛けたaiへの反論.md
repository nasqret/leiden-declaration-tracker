# 「数学は人間の営みである」——世界150人の数学者が静かに仕掛けたAIへの反論

> **Outlet:** [[note (数学の翻訳家)]] · **Type:** ✍️ Blogs · **Author:** 数学の翻訳家 (Math Translator) · **Date:** 2026-06-03 · **Language:** Japanese

**Link:** https://note.com/math_translator/n/n4ba2b9989a3c

## Summary

A Japanese blog post on note.com explaining the 2026 Leiden Declaration on AI and Mathematics, framing it as a quiet pushback by 150+ mathematicians against AI hype. It summarizes the declaration's five core threats (plausible-but-wrong proofs, attribution/copyright issues, AI use becoming an evaluation criterion, bypassing peer review via press releases, and research being distorted toward "AI-solvable" problems) and stresses the declaration's central message that mathematics must remain a fundamentally human endeavor.

## Key quote

> 「数学は人間の営みである」(Mathematics is a human endeavor / must remain fundamentally a human activity)

## Significance

Minor independent outlet. note.com is a popular Japanese self-publishing/blogging platform; "数学の翻訳家" (Math Translator) is a pseudonymous math-explainer account, not a notable mathematician or AI figure and not a major media outlet. Value is as evidence of Japanese-language public reception/popularization of the declaration rather than authoritative or high-reach coverage.

## Notes

Page fully accessible (not paywalled). Correctly references THIS 2026 declaration: IMU endorsement, Peter Scholze among signatories, and the five AI-related threats to mathematics — distinct from the 2015 Leiden Manifesto for research metrics. Note: the article says "150 mathematicians," which understates the 2,500+ total signatories cited in the brief, but it unambiguously concerns the correct declaration. Several other Japanese-language pieces on the same declaration surfaced in search (ビジネス+IT/SBBit, Yahoo News expert column, another note.com author mhamadajp).
