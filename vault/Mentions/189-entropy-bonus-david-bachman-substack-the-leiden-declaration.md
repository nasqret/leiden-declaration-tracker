# The Leiden Declaration

> **Outlet:** [[Entropy Bonus (David Bachman, Substack)]] · **Type:** 📨 Newsletters · **Author:** David Bachman · **Date:** 2026-06-23 · **Language:** English · **Country:** [[United States (country)\|United States]]

**Link:** https://profbachman.substack.com/p/the-leiden-declaration

## Summary

Original commentary post on David Bachman's "Entropy Bonus" Substack examining the 2026 Leiden Declaration, the call to action by mathematicians (from a September 2025 Leiden University conference) on the challenges AI poses to mathematics research. Bachman appreciates the declaration's emphasis on attribution but argues it prioritizes preserving mathematical culture over its core values. He raises unresolved tensions: how to credit human work when AI trains on the collective mathematical commons, and whether to accept machine-generated proofs that are formally verified yet incomprehensible to humans. He notes the authors hope it can be a model for other disciplines to adapt to their own values. Explicitly the 2026 AI-and-mathematics declaration, not the 2015 Leiden Manifesto for research metrics.

## Key quote

> Technologies that draw extensively on the published mathematical commons undermine the traditional system of attribution.
