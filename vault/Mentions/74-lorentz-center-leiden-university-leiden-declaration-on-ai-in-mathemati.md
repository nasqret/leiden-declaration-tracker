# Leiden Declaration on AI in Mathematics traces its origins to Lorentz Center workshop

> **Outlet:** [[Lorentz Center (Leiden University)]] · **Type:** 🏛️ Institutions & societies · **Author:** Lorentz Center (institutional, no individual byline identified) · **Date:** 2026-06 · **Language:** English · **Country:** [[Netherlands (country)\|Netherlands]]

**Link:** https://www.lorentzcenter.nl/leiden-declaration-on-ai-in-mathematics-traces-its-origins-to-lorentz-center-workshop.html

## Summary

The Lorentz Center's own announcement explains that the 2026 Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics originated at its September 2025 NIAS-Lorentz workshop "Mechanization and Mathematical Research," which gathered around 60 participants from 10 countries (mathematicians, computer scientists, philosophers, historians, social scientists). It notes that in the eight months following the conference a smaller working group developed the Declaration with extensive community feedback, and that the resulting Declaration has been endorsed by the International Mathematical Union.

## Key quote

> During the eight months following the conference, a smaller working group developed the Declaration, with extensive feedback from the mathematical community.

## Significance

High institutional relevance: this is the primary-source venue where the Declaration originated. The Lorentz Center (part of Leiden University) is a well-known international scientific workshop center, lending authoritative provenance to the Declaration's origin story, the September 2025 workshop, and the IMU endorsement.
