# Mathematicians say 'don't believe hype' on AI capabilities

> **Outlet:** [[Geo News (geo.tv, Pakistan)]] · **Type:** 📰 Newspapers & wire services · **Author:** AFP (Agence France-Presse) · **Date:** 2026-06-02 · **Language:** English

**Link:** https://www.geo.tv/latest/667041-mathematicians-say-dont-believe-hype-on-ai-capabilities

## Summary

An AFP wire report on the 2026 Leiden Declaration, which it says was signed by 150+ mathematics professors worldwide warning governments and the public not to "believe the hype" about AI's mathematical capabilities. It reports the declaration's concerns that AI labs have commercial incentives to overstate capabilities, that AI could produce plausible-but-incorrect proofs hard to verify, and that it could undermine attribution, peer review, and human oversight in mathematics.

## Key quote

> AI developers have "a strong commercial incentive... to overstate the capabilities of their products," and publicity can "misleadingly use specific mathematical tasks as metrics for the general reasoning capacities of commercial models."

## Significance

This is an AFP wire story, giving it broad international reach; it was syndicated to many outlets (Phys.org, Jamaica Observer, The Star, Manila Times, myRepublica). Geo News is one of Pakistan's largest news organizations. The piece quotes declaration co-author Michael Harris (Columbia University) and notes IMU vice-president Ulrike Tillmann endorsed it, and references Fields Medalist Terence Tao (cited by OpenAI). Notably, this AFP version does NOT name the Lorentz Center, Leiden University, or working-group lead Jim Portegies, and cites "150+" rather than 2,500+ signatories (figures at time of writing).

## Notes

Confirmed reference to THIS 2026 Leiden Declaration on AI and mathematics (not the 2015 Leiden Manifesto for research metrics): the article explicitly discusses AI, proofs, verification, attribution, peer review, the IMU, and Terence Tao. The Geo News page is a verbatim republication of the AFP story; identical text appears across multiple syndicating outlets, confirming authorship and content.
