## Summary

Russian-language news item (republished from Tochka.by on the regional Belarusian portal Virtual Brest) reporting that more than 150 mathematicians issued the Leiden Declaration urging governments and the public not to trust the hype around AI's claimed mathematical "breakthroughs." It explains the concerns: large language models can produce plausible-sounding but wrong reasoning, errors can propagate through chains of subsequent research, and the declaration (endorsed by the IMU) calls for less marketing noise, more open data, independent verification and accountability rather than abandoning AI.
