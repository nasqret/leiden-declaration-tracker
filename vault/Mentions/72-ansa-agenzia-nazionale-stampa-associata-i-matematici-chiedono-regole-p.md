## Summary

Original wire-service science article from ANSA, Italy's national news agency, reporting on the Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics. It explicitly attributes the declaration to 16 researchers from 15 European and American universities and notes the support of the International Mathematical Union (IMU). It enumerates the five threats the declaration identifies (plausible but unreliable arguments; lack of attribution/copyright violation; researcher dependence on costly technology; communication that overstates AI capabilities and understates human contribution; loss of research autonomy to commercial priorities) and raises ethical concerns about mathematicians' work being used to train AI for military/surveillance purposes. Peter Scholze (Max Planck director, 2018 Fields Medalist) is quoted on the goal of mathematical research being human understanding. Confirmed as the 2026 declaration, not the 2015 Leiden Manifesto.

## Key quote
