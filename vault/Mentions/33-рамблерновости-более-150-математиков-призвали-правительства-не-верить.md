# Более 150 математиков призвали правительства не верить ажиотажу вокруг искусственного интеллекта

> **Outlet:** [[Рамблер-новости]] · **Type:** 📰 Newspapers & wire services · **Author:** Naked-Science.ru (syndicated) · **Date:** 2026-06-10 · **Language:** Russian

**Link:** https://news.rambler.ru/tech/56585728-bolee-150-matematikov-prizvali-pravitelstva-ne-verit-azhiotazhu-vokrug-iskusstvennogo-intellekta/

## Summary

A Russian-language news aggregator article reporting that over 150 mathematicians published the "Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics," urging governments and policymakers to be cautious about AI hype and to consult mathematicians rather than rely on company press releases. It notes that AI-generated solutions may sound convincing but can contain errors that are hard to distinguish from valid proofs.

## Key quote

> Будущее математических исследований должны определять человеческие суждения, справедливые и прозрачные практики и общие ценности.

## Significance

Rambler is one of the largest Russian web portals/news aggregators, giving the declaration broad Russian-language reach. The content is syndicated from Naked-Science.ru, a popular Russian popular-science outlet. The article quotes IMU Vice-President Ulrike Tillmann and Oxford computer scientist Leslie Ann Goldberg, both notable figures, though the byline author is the outlet rather than a named expert.

## Notes

Confirmed reference to the 2026 Leiden Declaration on AI and Mathematics (not the 2015 Leiden Manifesto for research metrics). Article focuses on the policymaker-facing aspect. It cites the figure "more than 150 mathematicians," likely an early count from when the piece was written shortly after the 2 June 2026 publication; the declaration later reached 2,500+ signatories. Leiden University and the Lorentz Center workshop are not explicitly named in this particular article.
