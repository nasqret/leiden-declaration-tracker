## Summary

An Indonesian-language newspaper report on the 2026 Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics, in which mathematicians worldwide warn against overstated AI capability claims in mathematics. It notes the declaration's endorsement by the International Mathematical Union and its warning that AI can produce proofs that appear correct but are false and hard for humans to verify.

## Key quote

> AI developers have strong commercial incentives to exaggerate product capabilities; claims following market timelines rather than peer-reviewed science can mislead by using certain mathematical tasks as metrics of general reasoning ability.
