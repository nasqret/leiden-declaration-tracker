# Hàng trăm nhà toán học đã ký tuyên ngôn Leiden nhằm phản đối sự lạm dụng AI trong nghiên cứu

> **Outlet:** [[CafeF (republished from Người Đưa Tin)]] · **Type:** 📰 Newspapers & wire services · **Author:** Đức Khương · **Date:** 2026-06-04 · **Language:** Vietnamese · **Country:** [[Vietnam (country)\|Vietnam]]

**Link:** https://cafef.vn/hang-tram-nha-toan-hoc-da-ky-tuyen-ngon-leiden-nham-phan-doi-su-lam-dung-ai-trong-nghien-cuu-188260604161508108.chn

## Summary

Vietnamese-language news article reporting that hundreds of mathematicians worldwide (over 650 cited as of June 3) have signed the 2026 Leiden Declaration opposing the misuse of AI in mathematics research, warning that overreliance on AI threatens the humanistic nature of mathematics, peer-review integrity, and proper attribution. It quotes signatories and critics including Peter Scholze, Kevin Buzzard, and Leslie Ann Goldberg, and criticizes tech companies for using AI math capabilities as a publicity stunt to inflate stock valuations.

## Key quote

> AI-generated papers containing errors are like houses built on rotten foundations that will eventually collapse (paraphrasing Leslie Ann Goldberg, Oxford).

## Notes

Confirmed as referencing THIS 2026 Leiden Declaration on AI and Mathematics, not the 2015 Leiden Manifesto on research metrics: signatory names (Scholze, Buzzard), the Goldberg quote, transparency/attribution/peer-review themes, and criticism of tech-company hype all match. Article does not name Terence Tao or use the exact "don't believe the hype" phrasing, but conveys the same anti-hype warning.
