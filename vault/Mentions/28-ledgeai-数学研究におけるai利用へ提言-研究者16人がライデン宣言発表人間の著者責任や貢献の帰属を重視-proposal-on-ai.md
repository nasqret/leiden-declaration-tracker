# 数学研究におけるAI利用へ提言　研究者16人が「ライデン宣言」発表、人間の著者責任や貢献の帰属を重視 (Proposal on AI use in mathematical research: 16 researchers announce the "Leiden Declaration," emphasizing human author responsibility and attribution of contributions)

> **Outlet:** [[Ledge.ai]] · **Type:** 📰 Newspapers & wire services · **Author:** Ledge.ai 編集部 (Ledge.ai Editorial Team) · **Date:** 2026-06-07 · **Language:** Japanese

**Link:** https://ledge.ai/articles/leiden_declaration_ai_mathematics_author_responsibility

## Summary

News article in Japan's AI-focused news media Ledge.ai reporting that researchers (described as 16) announced the "Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics" on 2 June 2026. It explains that as AI-generated proofs and formalization advance, the declaration warns of impacts on proof reliability, attribution of authorship/contributions, research evaluation, and research autonomy, and offers recommendations to mathematicians, academic bodies, policymakers, and commercial AI companies. It notes the declaration is endorsed by the International Mathematical Union (IMU).

## Key quote

> 宣言が強調するのは、AIや自動化された手法を使ったとしても、研究成果の責任は人間の著者に残るという原則です。 (The declaration emphasizes the principle that responsibility for research results remains with the human authors, even when AI or automated methods are used.)

## Significance

Ledge.ai is one of Japan's largest AI-specialized news media outlets, giving the declaration meaningful reach in the Japanese-language AI/tech audience. The piece is bylined to the editorial team (no notable individual mathematician or AI figure as author). Notably, the article describes the working group as "16 researchers" affiliated with Leiden, Cambridge, Oxford, ETH Zurich, etc., and does not name Jim Portegies or marquee signatories like Tao or Scholze, but it accurately captures the declaration's core themes and IMU endorsement.
