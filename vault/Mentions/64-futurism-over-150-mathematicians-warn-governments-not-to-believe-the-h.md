# Over 150 Mathematicians Warn Governments Not to "Believe the Hype" About AI

> **Outlet:** [[Futurism]] · **Type:** 🧪 Science & tech magazines · **Author:** Victor Tangermann · **Date:** 2026-06-06 · **Language:** English · **Country:** [[United States (country)\|United States]]

**Link:** https://futurism.com/artificial-intelligence/mathematicians-warn-governments-hype-ai

## Summary

Futurism reports on the 2026 Leiden Declaration on AI and Mathematics, signed by 150+ mathematics experts, framing it as a warning to policymakers not to overstate AI's ability to solve mathematical problems (citing OpenAI's Erdos conjecture claims as context). It emphasizes the declaration's concerns about commercial incentives to exaggerate AI capabilities and about AI producing plausible-but-incorrect proofs.

## Key quote

> "Current automated techniques can produce plausible but unreliable (or even incorrect) arguments which are difficult to distinguish from correct mathematical proofs."
