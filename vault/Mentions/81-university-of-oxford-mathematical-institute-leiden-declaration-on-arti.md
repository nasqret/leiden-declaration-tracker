# Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics

> **Outlet:** [[University of Oxford Mathematical Institute]] · **Type:** 🏛️ Institutions & societies · **Author:** Mathematical Institute (University of Oxford); declaration co-authored by Ursula Martin (Oxford), working group convened by Jim Portegies (TU/e) · **Date:** 2026-06-02 · **Language:** English · **Country:** [[United Kingdom (country)\|United Kingdom]]

**Link:** https://www.maths.ox.ac.uk/node/81479

## Summary

The Oxford Mathematical Institute announces the release of the 2026 Leiden Declaration on AI and Mathematics, noting it was authored by 16 international mathematicians including Oxford's own Ursula Martin and endorsed by the IMU and Isaac Newton Institute. The page summarizes the declaration's concerns: unreliable AI results, copyright/attribution issues, dependence on expensive proprietary technology, AI hype, and loss of human autonomy over research agendas.

## Key quote

> The Declaration...focusses on the threats (and opportunities) that AI presents such as: unreliable results; copyright and attribution issues (AI using human work without citation); inequality and dependence on expensive proprietary technology; misleading hype and overestimation of AI capabilities; and loss of human autonomy over research agendas.

## Significance

Major institutional source: the University of Oxford's Mathematical Institute is one of the world's leading mathematics departments. Significance is heightened because Oxford professor Ursula Martin (a notable figure in mathematics and formal verification) is one of the 16 authors, making this an institutional endorsement from an author's home department rather than mere reporting.
