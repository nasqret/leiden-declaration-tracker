# Opinion: Biologists Should Articulate Their Position on AI

> **Outlet:** [[Undark Magazine]] · **Type:** 🧪 Science & tech magazines · **Author:** C. Brandon Ogbunu · **Date:** 2026-07-02 · **Language:** English · **Country:** [[United States (country)\|United States]]

**Link:** https://undark.org/2026/07/02/opinion-biologists-leiden-declaration/

## Summary

An opinion piece in Undark Magazine by C. Brandon Ogbunu (Yale) using the Leiden Declaration on AI and Mathematics as a model to argue that biologists should articulate their own position on AI. It contends biology is uniquely vulnerable because biological truths are 'noisy and context-dependent' rather than definitively verifiable, and proposes a biology declaration emphasising researcher accountability, lab validation of AI-generated hypotheses, and an adaptive, regularly updated structure.

## Key quote

> In June, a community of mostly mathematicians released the Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics, an articulation of the values they hope to preserve as automated systems are integrated into the practice of developing mathematical proofs.
