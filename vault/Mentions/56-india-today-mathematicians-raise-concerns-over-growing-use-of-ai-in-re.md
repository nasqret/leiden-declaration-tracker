## Summary

India Today's technology section reports on the Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics, describing how a group of mathematicians (led by Jim Portegies, endorsed by the International Mathematical Union) warn that the growing use of AI in mathematical research threatens the discipline's core values. The piece summarizes the declaration's concerns about AI-generated proofs of unreliable quality, risks to peer review and research integrity, unattributed use of mathematicians' work as training data, and commercial overstatement of AI capabilities ("don't believe the hype"), along with its recommendations to disclose AI use, ensure peer review, and level the playing field between academia and for-profit companies. This is the 2026 Leiden Declaration, not the 2015 Leiden Manifesto for research metrics.
