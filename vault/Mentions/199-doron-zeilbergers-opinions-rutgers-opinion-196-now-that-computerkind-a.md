# Opinion 196: Now that Computerkind (aka AI) is getting so much SMARTER than Humankind, let's hope that it will also get WISER, and chuck the outdated Platonic-Euclidean Human Way of Doing Mathematics

> **Outlet:** [[Doron Zeilberger's Opinions (Rutgers)]] · **Type:** ✍️ Blogs · **Author:** Doron Zeilberger · **Date:** 2026-06-15 · **Language:** English · **Country:** [[United States (country)\|United States]]

**Link:** https://sites.math.rutgers.edu/~zeilberg/Opinion196.html

## Summary

An essay in Doron Zeilberger's long-running 'Opinions' series (Rutgers) that references the Leiden Declaration on AI and Mathematics — which he learned about via Siobhan Roberts' New York Times article. Taking a contrarian stance, Zeilberger argues that as AI surpasses human mathematical ability, mathematics should abandon its insistence on absolute rigorous proof in favour of probabilistic mathematical knowledge — a counterpoint to the declaration's defence of proof, rigour and human oversight.

## Key quote

> Thanks to Siobhan Roberts' fascinating article ... I learned about the Leiden declaration.
