# "there's this like massive exogenous force" haunting mathematics

> **Outlet:** [[Silicon Reckoner]] · **Type:** 📨 Newsletters · **Author:** Michael Harris · **Date:** 2026-06-13 · **Language:** English · **Country:** [[United States (country)\|United States]]

**Link:** https://siliconreckoner.substack.com/p/theres-this-like-massive-exogenous

## Summary

Michael Harris, a Columbia number theorist, critiques how the media framed the June 2026 Leiden Declaration on AI and Mathematics as a fearful "cry for help" prompted by OpenAI's announcement of solving an Erdős conjecture. He argues the Declaration's release had been planned over a month in advance, that the OpenAI press release and the Declaration were independent events falsely turned into a cause-and-effect narrative, and that the real concerns are about corporate control, intellectual property, exploited labor, and consolidation of power rather than anxiety over automation.

## Key quote

> Mathematics is a rich form of cultural expression...what I am worried about is that a handful of corporations are mobilizing their vast financial resources to impose an impoverished view (quoting Rodrigo Ochigame)
