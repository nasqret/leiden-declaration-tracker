## Summary

Thai-language news report (sourced from Reuters, datelined Paris, June 3) on the Leiden Declaration on AI and Mathematics, in which mathematicians worldwide urge governments not to believe exaggerated hype about AI's mathematical capabilities. It cites concerns that developers have commercial incentives to overstate abilities, that benchmarks are misrepresented as general reasoning, and that AI can produce plausible-looking but flawed proofs, alongside attribution risks.
