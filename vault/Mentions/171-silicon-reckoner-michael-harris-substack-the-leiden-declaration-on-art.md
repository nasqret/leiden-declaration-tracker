# The Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics

> **Outlet:** [[Silicon Reckoner (Michael Harris, Substack)]] · **Type:** 📨 Newsletters · **Author:** Michael Harris · **Date:** 2026-06-02 · **Language:** English · **Country:** [[United States (country)\|United States]]

**Link:** https://siliconreckoner.substack.com/p/the-leiden-declaration-on-artificial

## Summary

Michael Harris's Silicon Reckoner post covers the newly released 2026 Leiden Declaration on AI and Mathematics, describing how an international group of mathematicians warns that AI poses five major threats to mathematics (unreliable published results, improper attribution, inequality among researchers, overhyping of capabilities, and loss of disciplinary autonomy). It notes the declaration's endorsement by the International Mathematical Union and its call for action by researchers, institutions, and governments.

## Key quote

> AI is fundamentally transforming the way we do mathematics, and it is doing so at incredible speed. It offers fantastic possibilities when used honestly and competently as a 'research assistant'. (quoting Ilka Agricola, IMU Committee on Publishing chair)
