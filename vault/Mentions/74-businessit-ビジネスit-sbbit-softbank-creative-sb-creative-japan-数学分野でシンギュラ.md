## Summary

A Japanese business/IT magazine feature arguing that mathematics may be reaching a "singularity" as cutting-edge AI (e.g., GPT-5.4 Pro) proves a string of previously unsolved problems and even discovers novel structural methods. It frames the 2026 "Leiden Declaration" (ライデン宣言) as an "emergency statement" issued by 16 researchers from universities and institutions across 15 countries, expressing mathematicians' concern over the paradigm shift in math and the changing roles of AI and humans, and calling for disclosure of AI use and accountability by authors.

## Key quote

> 世界15カ国の大学や研究機関に所属する16人の研究者が「ライデン宣言」を発表した。緊急声明ともいえる「ライデン宣言」 (Sixteen researchers from universities and institutions across 15 countries issued the "Leiden Declaration"... a "Leiden Declaration" that can be called an emergency statement.)
