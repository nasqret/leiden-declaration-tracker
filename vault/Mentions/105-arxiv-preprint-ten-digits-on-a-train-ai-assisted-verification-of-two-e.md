# Ten Digits on a Train: AI-Assisted Verification of Two Eigenvalue Problems

## Summary

arXiv preprint (2606.23821v1, math.NA, submitted 22 June 2026) by Matthew J. Colbrook (DAMTP, University of Cambridge) reporting a human-AI collaboration to certify eigenvalues for a singular Schrodinger operator and a non-normal atom-molecule benchmark to ten decimal places, using ChatGPT 5.5 with Codex as an exploratory assistant. In its discussion of verification, attribution, and peer review in AI-assisted mathematics, the paper explicitly invokes the Leiden Declaration: "The community is beginning to say this explicitly. The Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics, published on June 2, 2026, and endorsed by the International Mathematical Union, emphasizes verification, attribution, disclosure, research autonomy, funding, and unequal access [3]." Reference [3] gives the full author list (Alper, Barany, Chavarri Villarello, Dahmen, Dean, Ganapathy, Harris, Holmes, Jamnik, Kelk, Kra, Martin, Naskrecki, Ochigame, Portegies, Schmitt).
