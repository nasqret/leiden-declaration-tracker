# Mathematicians call for action on AI to protect the future of the discipline

> **Outlet:** [[Isaac Newton Institute]] · **Type:** 🏛️ Institutions & societies · **Author:** Isaac Newton Institute (institutional newsroom; no individual byline; features comments from Director Prof. Ulrike Tillmann FRS) · **Date:** 2026-06 · **Language:** English

**Link:** https://www.newton.ac.uk/news/ini-news/mathematicians-call-for-action-on-ai-to-protect-the-future-of-the-discipline/

## Summary

Institutional news article from the Isaac Newton Institute for Mathematical Sciences (Cambridge) reporting on the 2026 Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics, which grew out of a 2025 Lorentz Center workshop in Leiden and is endorsed by the International Mathematical Union. It explains that the declaration calls on mathematicians, research organisations and governments to act to protect the core values of mathematics (proof, peer review, attribution, openness) in the age of AI, and notes the declaration does not call for an outright ban but recommends disclosing AI assistance and upholding peer review. The INI states it was proud to have played a small but important role in developing the declaration.

## Key quote

> A new international declaration is calling on mathematicians, research organisations and governments to act now to protect the core values of mathematics in the age of artificial intelligence.

## Significance

High institutional weight: the Isaac Newton Institute is one of the world's leading mathematical sciences research institutes (University of Cambridge). The piece quotes INI Director Prof. Ulrike Tillmann FRS, a prominent mathematician and IMU vice president, lending notable disciplinary authority; the INI also states it contributed to the declaration's development.
