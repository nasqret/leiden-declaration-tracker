## Summary

Kwantowo, a long-running Polish popular-science blog authored by Adam Adamczyk (focused on physics, astronomy and rationalism), published a feature-length article on the 2026 Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics. The piece explicitly identifies the declaration as published 2 June 2026, initiated by Jim Portegies of Eindhoven University of Technology, and signed by 2,700+ scientists. It frames the context as OpenAI's May 2026 claim of solving the unit-distance geometry problem, which sparked debate over AI's role in mathematics. The article lays out five core concerns of the declaration: reduced proof credibility (AI produces convincing but potentially flawed arguments), blurred authorship, distorted research motivation (topics chosen for AI compatibility over importance), marketing-driven publication before peer review, and loss of research autonomy to tech companies. It reports the declaration's practical proposals: mandatory disclosure of AI assistance, stronger reviewer controls, and public investment in computing infrastructure so independent researchers can verify theorems, keeping mathematics part of open science. This is an original Polish-language article, not a machine translation or mirror.
