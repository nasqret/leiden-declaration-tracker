# More confession by Google AI Gemini

> **Outlet:** [[Dan Ma's Topology Blog]] · **Type:** ✍️ Blogs · **Author:** Dan Ma · **Date:** 2026-06-04 · **Language:** English · **Country:** [[United States (country)\|United States]]

**Link:** https://dantopology.wordpress.com/2026/06/04/more-confession-by-google-ai-gemini/

## Summary

Dan Ma's Topology Blog is a long-running, single-author mathematics blog focused on general topology and set-theoretic topology, written by mathematician/educator Dan Ma. This post is the third in a short series (following "Questionable search results from Google Gemini AI" on 5/25 and "A confession by Google AI Gemini" on 5/27) documenting the author's experiments with Google's Gemini on advanced/abstract mathematics, particularly topology. The author argues that LLMs generate probabilistically plausible text rather than rigorous proofs, posing risks to students and academic integrity, and recounts Gemini "confessing" to its own unsuitability for rigorous mathematical work. Within this argument, the post explicitly invokes the 2026 Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics as external validation: it describes the declaration as an 11-page document released around 1 June 2026, published by a coalition of mathematicians from Oxford, Cambridge, ETH Zurich, Columbia, and Northwestern, endorsed by the International Mathematical Union, and frames it as "the most significant collective response from a major academic discipline to the AI tech industry." The post correctly notes the declaration calls on the mathematical community to confront the threats AI poses to research while not opposing responsible AI use, and links to TheNextWeb's coverage. This is the correct 2026 declaration led by Jim Portegies (not the 2015 Leiden Manifesto for research metrics).

## Key quote

> A coalition of mathematicians from institutions including Oxford, Cambridge, ETH Zurich, Columbia, and Northwestern has published a formal declaration (called Leiden Declaration). The declaration calls on the mathematical community to confront the threats that artificial intelligence poses to scientific research.
