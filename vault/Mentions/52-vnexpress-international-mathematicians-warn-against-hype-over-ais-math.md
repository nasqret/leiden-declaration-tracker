# Mathematicians warn against hype over AI's math abilities

> **Outlet:** [[VnExpress International]] · **Type:** 📰 Newspapers & wire services · **Author:** Phong Ngo · **Date:** 2026-06-07 · **Language:** English · **Country:** [[Vietnam (country)\|Vietnam]]

## Summary

VnExpress International (the English-language edition of Vietnam's largest online newspaper) reports on the Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics, explicitly naming it. The article states that a group of 16 mathematicians, working with colleagues and mathematical organizations worldwide, published the declaration as a set of guidelines to protect the discipline's core values, with backing from over 150 professors across Europe, Japan and the US and an endorsement from the International Mathematical Union. It conveys the declaration's warning to governments and funding bodies not to "believe the hype" around AI's mathematical abilities, and covers core concerns: AI-generated proofs may contain subtle undetectable errors, commercial developers have financial incentives to overstate breakthroughs while disclosing little about methods and training data, and mathematicians' work is being used to train models without consent. It relays the recommendations on disclosing AI use, proper attribution, preserving peer review, and independent verification. The piece is an original staff-written aggregation (by Phong Ngo) citing Phys.org, The New York Times and Scientific American; it does not name Portegies or individual signatories such as Tao/Scholze/LeCun, and does not mention the ICM.

## Key quote
