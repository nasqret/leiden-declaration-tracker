# Leiden Declaration: commercial AI is a threat to the publishing and rewards system in mathematics

> **Outlet:** [[chorasimilarity (Marius Buliga blog)]] · **Type:** ✍️ Blogs · **Author:** Marius Buliga (chorasimilarity) · **Date:** 2026-06-04 · **Language:** English · **Country:** [[Romania (country)\|Romania]]

## Summary

A personal-blog post by mathematician Marius Buliga (a senior researcher known for sub-Riemannian geometry, convex analysis, and the chemlambda computational model) on his long-running "chorasimilarity" WordPress open notebook. The post explicitly engages with the 2026 Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics (linking to leidendeclaration.ai/#declaration as its source) and offers original, skeptical commentary rather than mere summary. Buliga reframes the declaration's worries: he argues the real thing being threatened is "the symbiotic pair formed by the academic publication business and academic management" (the publishing-and-rewards system), and is critical of fears about non-legacy/commercial-AI "publications", viewing them as defending an outdated system rather than defending mathematics itself. He counters that more serious threats are "algorithmic bureaucracy", institutional misuse of AI tools, potential defunding, and the erosion of the value of human peer review. The piece is consistent with related posts on the same blog (e.g. "A CAPTCHA for scientific publications", 2026-06-13) and fits Buliga's standing interest in scholarly publishing reform.

## Key quote

> the mathematical community's leadership is primarily threatened by disruption to the symbiotic pair formed by the academic publication business and academic management
