# Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics

> **Outlet:** [[Wikipedia]] · **Type:** 🔗 Other · **Author:** Wikipedia contributors (collaborative, no single byline) · **Date:** 2026-06-16 · **Language:** English

**Link:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leiden_Declaration_on_Artificial_Intelligence_and_Mathematics

## Summary

English-language Wikipedia article dedicated to the 2026 Leiden Declaration, describing it as a statement (published 2 June 2026) by an international group of mathematicians warning that reliance on AI-generated proofs threatens the accuracy, reliability and independent verifiability of mathematical research. It notes the declaration's origin at the September 2025 Lorentz Center workshop at Leiden University, its endorsement by the International Mathematical Union, leadership by Jim Portegies, and its calls to disclose AI use, uphold peer review and protect the discipline's values.

## Key quote

> Technology companies' involvement in research raises the risk that research questions are prioritized... because of their amenability to AI methods rather than their deeper significance.
