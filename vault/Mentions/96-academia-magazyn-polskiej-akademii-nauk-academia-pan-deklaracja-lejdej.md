# Deklaracja Lejdejska. Matematycy wyznaczają granice AI

> **Outlet:** [[Academia. Magazyn Polskiej Akademii Nauk (Academia PAN)]] · **Type:** 🧪 Science & tech magazines · **Date:** 2026-06-26 · **Language:** Polish · **Country:** [[Poland (country)\|Poland]]

## Summary

Article on the popular-science portal of the Polish Academy of Sciences (PAN) covering the Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics. It explains the declaration was created after OpenAI announced (May 2026) that its AI solved the famous unit-distance problem in geometry, prompting mathematicians to draft rules protecting the field from opaque proofs and commercial pressure rather than reject AI outright. It reports the declaration demands transparency, disclosure of AI use, disclosure of training-data sources, protection of authorship, and human accountability for correctness of results, while acknowledging AI's potential for discovering results, supporting proof formalization, and building research tools. States over 2,600 scientists support it, endorsed by the International Mathematical Union (which plans to present it at the IMU conference), with backing from Terence Tao, Peter Scholze, and Robbert Dijkgraaf. Explicitly this 2026 declaration, not the 2015 Leiden Manifesto.
