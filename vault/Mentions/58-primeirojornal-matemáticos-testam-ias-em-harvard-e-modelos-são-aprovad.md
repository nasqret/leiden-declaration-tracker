# Matemáticos testam IAs em Harvard e modelos são aprovados em 7 dos 10 problemas

> **Outlet:** [[PrimeiroJornal]] · **Type:** 📰 Newspapers & wire services · **Author:** Olhar Digital (republished/credited) · **Date:** 2026-06-14 · **Language:** Portuguese · **Country:** [[Brazil (country)\|Brazil]]

**Link:** https://primeirojornal.com.br/2026/06/14/matematicos-testam-ias-em-harvard-e-modelos-sao-aprovados-em-7-dos-10-problemas/

## Summary

Brazilian news aggregator article (republishing an Olhar Digital piece) reporting on the Harvard "First Proof" project, where 30 mathematicians tested AI models on 10 advanced problems and 7 received at least one correct AI solution (mainly GPT-5.5 Pro, with Gemini 3.1 Pro as alternative). In parallel, the article cites the 2026 Leiden Declaration as the mathematical community's ethical-guidelines response, noting 2,300+ signatories and concerns about transparent AI use in mathematics.

## Key quote

> Paralelamente, a comunidade lançou a Declaração de Leiden, com mais de 2.300 signatários, que traça diretrizes éticas para uso transparente da IA na matemática. O documento reconhece o potencial da tecnologia, mas aponta riscos como a falta de crédito às ideias utilizadas pelos modelos e a promessa de resultados sem transparência sobre falhas.

## Notes

Confirmed reference to THIS 2026 Leiden Declaration on AI and Mathematics (not the 2015 Leiden Manifesto for research metrics): explicitly "Declaração de Leiden," cites 2,300+ signatories, ethical guidelines for transparent AI use in mathematics, and risks around attribution/credit and transparency — matching the subject. Signatory count (2,300+) is slightly lower than the 2,500+ figure, consistent with an earlier snapshot.
