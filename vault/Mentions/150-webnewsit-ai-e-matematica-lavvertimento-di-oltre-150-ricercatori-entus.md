# AI e matematica, l'avvertimento di oltre 150 ricercatori: entusiasmo sì, ma la scienza non si fa per comunicato stampa

> **Outlet:** [[Webnews.it]] · **Type:** ✍️ Blogs · **Author:** Silvia Dalia · **Date:** 2026-06-12 · **Language:** Italian · **Country:** [[Italy (country)\|Italy]]

**Link:** https://www.webnews.it/ai-e-matematica-lavvertimento-di-oltre-150-ricercatori-entusiasmo-si-ma-la-scienza-non-si-fa-per-comunicato-stampa/

## Summary

Italian tech news article covering the 2026 Leiden Declaration on AI and Mathematics, reporting that over 150 mathematicians and theoretical computer scientists signed a call for safeguards on AI in mathematical research, stressing that scientific discoveries require independent peer review rather than corporate press announcements. It discusses AI-generated proofs, hallucinations, attribution problems, and concentration of computational power among tech companies, using the OpenAI-Erdős conjecture case as an example.

## Key quote

> l'entusiasmo per i nuovi strumenti non può prendere il posto della verifica indipendente

Page fetched and confirmed to reference THIS 2026 Leiden Declaration on AI and Mathematics (mentions Leiden, the declaration, IMU/Ulrike Tillmann, and multiple named signatories). Not the 2015 Leiden Manifesto for research metrics. Article does not mention Lorentz Center, Jim Portegies, or the Tao/Scholze signatories by name, but core subject identification is unambiguous.
