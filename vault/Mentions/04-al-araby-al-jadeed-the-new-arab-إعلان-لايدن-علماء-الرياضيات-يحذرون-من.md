## Summary

Arabic-language article in the pan-Arab daily Al-Araby Al-Jadeed / The New Arab reporting on the 2026 Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics. It states that 16 mathematicians from leading universities (Washington, Amsterdam, Edinburgh, Warwick, California, Columbia, Leiden, Cambridge, Maastricht, Northwestern, Oxford, Warsaw, Eindhoven, Zurich) issued the declaration to frame the debate on AI's impact on mathematics. It quotes the declaration's warning that current automated tools can "produce seemingly plausible but unreliable (or even incorrect) arguments that are difficult to distinguish from correct mathematical proofs," and raises concerns about copyright exploitation of mathematicians' work, threats to the independence of mathematics, unequal collaboration between researchers and tech companies, and companies overstating their tools' capabilities. It notes the declaration does not call for rejecting AI, acknowledging useful applications (exploring ideas, checking cases, suggesting counterexamples), and cites context such as OpenAI announcing in late May that one of its models refuted a prominent 80-year-old conjecture in combinatorial geometry.
