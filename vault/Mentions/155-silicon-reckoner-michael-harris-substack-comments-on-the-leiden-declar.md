# Comments on: The Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics

> **Outlet:** [[Silicon Reckoner (Michael Harris, Substack)]] · **Type:** 📨 Newsletters · **Author:** Michael Harris · **Date:** 2026-06-02 · **Language:** English · **Country:** [[United States (country)\|United States]]

**Link:** https://siliconreckoner.substack.com/p/the-leiden-declaration-on-artificial/comments

## Summary

The post on Michael Harris's "Silicon Reckoner" Substack announces the June 2, 2026 Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics and reproduces its press release; the comments thread debates the declaration's concerns about AI-generated proofs being hard to verify, drawing parallels to the abc conjecture proof controversy and the risk of theorems being assigned probabilities rather than definitive proof status. Harris, himself a working-group author of the declaration, engages directly in the comments, expressing skepticism about AI being "transformative" for mathematical practice.

## Key quote

> I certainly don't think the word 'transformative' is at all appropriate.
