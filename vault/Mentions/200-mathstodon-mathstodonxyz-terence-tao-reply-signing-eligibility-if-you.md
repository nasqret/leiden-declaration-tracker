## Summary

A Mastodon reply by mathematician Terence Tao on his verified Mathstodon account (@tao@mathstodon.xyz), responding to user Colin Wright (@ColinTheMathmo) about who is eligible to sign the 2026 Leiden Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Mathematics. Colin (a PhD in pure mathematics not active in research for ~35 years) asked whether he qualified to sign; Tao replied that there is no requirement to be a practising research mathematician, and that if you consider yourself a stakeholder that is sufficient justification to sign. This is a thread continuation of Tao's parent post (116681023979910132) announcing the declaration is live and urging people to read and consider signing it. The reply explicitly concerns this 2026 declaration (endorsed by the IMU), not the 2015 Leiden Manifesto for research metrics. Original, first-party source authored by Tao himself; not a mirror.
